# Strategy Foundations

Most SocialSeal skills assume the brand already has a social strategy and a working SocialSeal setup. Many users do not, and the concepts SocialSeal relies on are not universally understood. Do not stall waiting for the user to supply these. Explain each one, then offer to define it with the user using SocialSeal research. Guiding means teaching the concept and proposing a concrete way to produce it, not asking "please input your personas."

## The foundations

### Target persona
Who the content is for: the specific viewer whose search and hesitation the content answers. A persona is not a demographic label ("women 25-34"); it is a viewer with a job-to-be-done, a context, and hesitations ("first-time solo traveler deciding whether a city is safe and worth it").

- Why it matters: personas decide which search intents and content jobs are worth pursuing.
- Derive with SocialSeal: run a search journey on the category/topic and read which questions and content jobs actually surface; cluster the keywords; infer the viewers behind those searches. Propose 2-4 personas grounded in surfaced search behavior, then confirm with the user.

### Content pillar
A durable theme the brand will keep making content about, tied to real search demand. 3-5 pillars, each mapping to a cluster of viewer jobs and keywords (e.g. "first-timer planning", "cost and value", "neighborhood guides").

- Why it matters: pillars are the unit that maps to tracking groups and to blueprint scopes.
- Derive with SocialSeal: cluster the tracked keyword set and surfaced content; group recurring viewer jobs into candidate pillars; validate each pillar has search demand and a presence gap worth filling.

### Brand voice
How the brand sounds and what it will and will not say: tone, reading level, point of view, and hard constraints (claims it cannot make, words to avoid). For UGC/creator content this is creator-led and social-native, not ad copy.

- Why it matters: voice keeps briefs and generated assets on-brand and compliant.
- Derive with SocialSeal: not a SocialSeal output. Ask for existing brand guidelines; if none, draft a short voice note from how the brand currently talks and from the tone of high-surfacing creator content in the category, then confirm.

### Goal / measurement intent
What success looks like in SocialSeal terms: improved discoverability or share of voice for which pillars, keywords, market, and platform, by when.

- Why it matters: without a measurable goal, a plan is a calendar, not a strategy.
- Derive with SocialSeal: pick the priority pillars/keywords and state the current measured baseline (discoverability/SOV) so movement is checkable.

## SocialSeal setup concepts (plain language)

- **Workspace:** the container for one brand/client/project. Everything is scoped to it.
- **Tracking group:** one measurement surface = one platform + one market/language + one coherent search intent (e.g. `TikTok / US / category searches`). Clean groups are what make discoverability and share of voice trustworthy.
- **Tracking item:** one tracked thing inside a group, usually a search keyword with region/platform.
- **Search journey:** a keyword-expansion / evidence run used to discover and validate keywords before or after building groups.
- **Opportunity:** a place to create or improve content because people search and current surfaced content leaves a gap.
- **Content job / lens:** what a piece of content does for the viewer (teach, compare, plan, reassure, show mood) and whether it is aspirational or utility/practical. See `content-lenses.md`.
- **Discoverability / share of voice:** how often and how prominently an entity surfaces for the tracked set. See `metrics-glossary.md`.

## Readiness signals

Strategy is ready enough to start production when:
- there are named personas tied to viewer jobs (not just demographics)
- there are 3-5 content pillars, each with real tracked search demand
- there is a brand voice / constraints note
- there is at least one measurable goal (pillar/keyword + market + platform + baseline)

SocialSeal setup is ready when:
- the right workspace is selected
- tracking groups exist that are clean (one platform, one market/language, one intent) and cover the priority pillars
- groups have tracking items and a baseline run, and exports produce rows (or the empty reason is understood)

When a foundation is missing, name it, explain it in one or two sentences, and propose the SocialSeal-backed way to define it. Then route into the relevant skill (`socialseal-workspace-setup`, `socialseal-tracking-group-design`, `socialseal-opportunity-analysis`) rather than waiting for a perfect brief.
